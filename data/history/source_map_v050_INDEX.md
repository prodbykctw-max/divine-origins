## v0.5.0 additions overview

### Seven new traditions

1. **Buddhist** — Theravada / Mahayana / Vajrayana. Tier-1 anchored on Adi-Buddha (Samantabhadra / Vajradhara) and Dharmakaya; Tier-2 on the Sambhogakaya Buddhas and Avalokiteshvara; Tier-3 on Mara as archonic adversary; cross-tier on Maitreya as returning savior and Padmasambhava as tantric culture-bringer.

2. **Zoroastrian** — Mazdayasna. Critical for understanding the historical influence on Second Temple Judaism, Gnosticism, and Christianity. Includes Ahura Mazda, Ahriman, the six Amesha Spentas, Saoshyant (earliest fully-developed eschatological savior), Yima (the Iranian PIE *Yemo reflex), Zurvan (Time as parent of twins in the Zurvanite heresy), the daevas (with the deva-inversion documented), and Mithra-Iranian.

3. **Taoist** — Chinese philosophical Taoism + folk religion. Tao as Tier-1 unnamable source; Three Pure Ones (Sanqing) as triad; Jade Emperor administering the celestial bureaucracy; Pangu as primordial-giant convergent with Ymir/Puruṣa; Xiwangmu as Queen Mother of the West; Laozi as divinized descended sage.

4. **Yoruba / Ifá** — West African + diaspora. Olodumare as withdrawn Tier-1; the Orishas as emanations (Obatala, Yemoja, Orunmila, Eshu/Elegua, Shango, Ogun); structurally parallel to El + sons-of-El, Monad + Aeons, Idjair + Wandjinas patterns.

5. **Andean** — Inca and pre-Inca. Viracocha as creator who withdrew westward across the Pacific promising to return; Inti as sun-ancestor of the Inca royal line; Pachamama as continuously-venerated Earth Mother; Illapa as thunder-warrior; Supay as ambivalent underworld-lord forcibly conflated with the Devil.

6. **Polynesian** — Hawaiian / Māori / Tahitian / Samoan / Tongan. Io as supreme uncreated source (contested provenance); Rangi-Papa primordial couple; Tāne as separator of sky and earth (convergent with Pangu); Tangaroa as ocean god; Māui as trickster-culture-hero (fire-bringer, island-fisher, sun-snarer, immortality-failer); Hina as moon/night/death.

7. **Slavic** — pre-Christian. Perun as direct PIE *Perkwunos cognate at head of Vladimir's pantheon (980 CE); Veles as underworld/cattle/poetry adversary in the storm-vs-serpent cosmic drama; Svarog as sky-father-smith; Dazhbog as sun-ancestor of the Russians; Mokosh as the only goddess in Vladimir's pantheon; Rod as ancestral fate-power.

## Two new cosmological frameworks

### 1. The Dragon-Slayer Formula

Anchored on Calvert Watkins's reconstruction (*How to Kill a Dragon*, 1995) of the inherited PIE poetic formula. 16 instances documented across:

- PIE root (*Perkwunos / *Tritós)
- Vedic (Indra / Vritra)
- Iranian (Thraētaona / Aži Dahāka)
- Hittite (Tarhunna / Illuyanka)
- Greek (Zeus / Typhon; Apollo / Python; Heracles / Hydra-Geryon-Cacus)
- Norse (Thor / Jörmungandr)
- Slavic (Perun / Veles)
- Celtic-Irish (Lugh / Balor)
- Mesopotamian (Marduk / Tiamat; Ninurta / Anzu)
- Egyptian (Set/Ra / Apep)
- Canaanite (Baal / Yam)
- Christian apocalyptic (Michael / Dragon)
- Convergent: Andean Illapa; Yoruba Shango

Scholarly anchors include Watkins, Lincoln, West, Katz.

### 2. The Dying-and-Rising God Formula

Uses the restricted version defended by Mettinger and Mark S. Smith — not Frazer's overextended category. Seven well-attested instances:

- Egyptian: Osiris (with Isis as mourner-restorer)
- Canaanite: Baal (with Anat as mourner)
- Mesopotamian: Inanna/Ishtar (descent and substitution)
- Greek: Persephone (with Demeter); Dionysus-Zagreus (Orphic dismemberment)
- Norse: Baldr (mistletoe death; return after Ragnarök)
- Christian: Christ (crucifixion/resurrection)

Also explicitly documents NON-instances (Krishna, Heracles, Buddha) for definitional clarity. Scholarly anchors: Frazer (overextended original), J. Z. Smith (critical narrowing), Mettinger and M. S. Smith (defended restricted version).
