## 1. The Danu cluster — the strongest IE goddess-cognate

PIE *deh₂nu- ("flowing waters / river") gives:

| Reflex | Tradition | Role |
|---|---|---|
| *Deh₂nu | Proto-Indo-European | Primordial water-mother (reconstructed) |
| Dānu | Vedic Sanskrit | Mother of Vṛtra and the Dānavas (RV 1.32) |
| Danu / Danann | Old Irish | Mother of the Tuatha Dé Danann |
| dānu | Avestan | River |
| Don, Dnieper, Dniester, Donets, Danube | Hydronyms | The goddess-name preserved in river-names across IE Europe |

**Note the inversion:** in the Vedic tradition the Dānavas are the *defeated* race, the asuras opposed to the devas. In the Celtic tradition the Tuath Dé Danann are the *victorious* race against the Fomorians. Same root mother-name, opposite alignment — a phenomenon that David Frawley reads as Indra's victory over the Dānavas being remembered with opposite valence in the Celtic west than in the Vedic east.

Canonical parallels in the database: **p200, p201, p202, p208**.

## 3. The Anakim / Iy-ʿanaq / Rephaim / Og / Bashan / Mt Hermon convergence

This is the most archaeologically anchored cluster in the new data. Multiple independent traditions place giant-warrior cult and divine-rebel descent in **the same geography** (Bashan / Golan / Mt Hermon / Hebron):

- **Ugaritic Rephaim Texts** (c. 13th c. BCE): the rpum (deified royal dead, with giant-warrior aspect) are summoned in ritual feasts. Geography: Bashan / Mt Hermon.
- **Egyptian Execration Texts** (c. 19th–18th c. BCE): the Iy-ʿanaq are cursed as enemies of pharaoh. Geography: southern Canaan / Hebron region.
- **Hebrew Bible**: Og of Bashan is "the last of the Rephaim" (Deut 3:11); the Anakim are at Hebron (Num 13). Deut 2:11 identifies Anakim as a subset of Rephaim.
- **1 Enoch 6**: the Watchers descend on Mt Hermon and beget the Nephilim. Same mountain.
- **Talmud Niddah 61a**: makes Og the grandson of Shemhazai the fallen Watcher — explicitly threading the post-flood Rephaim line back into the pre-flood Watcher line.

The Egyptian Iy-ʿanaq attestation is the strongest extra-biblical evidence for the historicity of the giant-clan tradition (Albright 1928; Mazar 1949). The three Iy-ʿanaq rulers in the Execration Texts bear Semitic names; the three sons of Anak (Sheshai, Ahiman, Talmai) at Hebron are Hurrian by name affinities — a complication noted in the scholarly literature but not generally taken as falsifying the identification.

Canonical parallels: **p212, p213, p214, p215, p216**.

## 5. The cosmogonic-sacrifice pattern (*Manu / *Yemo)

Bruce Lincoln (1975) reconstructed the PIE cosmogony: **Manu** (first priest) sacrifices **Yemo** (his twin), and from the dismembered body of Yemo, the cosmos and the social orders are made. Yemo, as the first to die, becomes the king of the dead.

| First Priest | Sacrificed Twin | Cosmos from Body | Tradition |
|---|---|---|---|
| *Manu | *Yemo | (reconstructed) | Proto-Indo-European |
| Manu | Yama | (the Puruṣa Sūkta version: gods sacrifice Puruṣa) | Vedic |
| (Mannus) | Ymir | Body becomes the earth | Norse |
| — | Yima | (degraded reflex) | Iranian/Avestan |
| — | Tiamat | Body becomes heaven and earth | Babylonian (non-IE but parallel) |
| — | Christ (typological) | Cosmic-Christ as new Adam | Christian (developmental) |

Canonical parallels: **p221, p222**.

The Vedic Puruṣa Sūkta (RV 10.90) is the most explicit datable attestation of this pattern. Yama as "first to die, king of the dead" preserves *Yemo's identity directly.

## 6. The "Origins of Evil" framework

A new conceptual framework added under `cosmological_frameworks`. It tracks the reception-history of **Genesis 6:1-4** across two millennia:

**Seed text**: Gen 6:1-4 (sons of God, daughters of men, Nephilim).

**Core developments**:
1. *1 Enoch 1–36 (Book of the Watchers)*: the cryptic Genesis passage is expanded into a full cosmology. 200 angels under Shemhazai descend on Mt Hermon. Asael / Azazel teaches forbidden arts (metallurgy, weapons, cosmetics, sorcery). The Nephilim devour creation. Their spirits, after the flood, become the demons of the present age (1 Enoch 15).
2. *Jubilees, Dead Sea Scrolls (Book of Giants), 2 Peter, Jude*: develops the framework in different sectarian and early-Christian forms.
3. *Gnostic re-reading*: the Watchers become the archonic enforcers of Yaldabaoth; their forbidden teachings are the chains of material captivity (*On the Origin of the World*; *Apocryphon of John*).
4. *Manichaean adaptation*: the Watchers reveal heavenly mysteries to humanity, but in a form that entangles rather than liberates (*Kephalaia 92*).

**Cross-tradition parallels** (built into the framework metadata):
- Greek: Prometheus's gift of fire to humanity, followed by his binding (structurally identical to Asael; valence-inverted)
- Vedic: Dānavas and Daityas descended from Kashyapa-rishi through Dānu and Diti
- Celtic: Fomorians at Mag Tuired
- Mesopotamian: the apkallu (antediluvian wise beings) and the older gods slain by Marduk
- Canaanite: the rpum cult of Bashan / Mt Hermon

**Significance**: This framework is the alternative answer (within Judeo-Christian tradition) to the Adam-and-Eve account of evil's origin. Where Gen 3 locates evil in human disobedience, the Watcher tradition locates it in angelic rebellion and the technological / biological corruption of humanity *from above*. Rabbinic Judaism and Pauline Christianity largely suppressed the Watcher account in favor of the Adamic; Gnostic and Manichaean traditions developed the Watcher account into full cosmologies. For comparative purposes, the Watcher framework is the bridge that connects the biblical Anakim / Rephaim / Nephilim to the IE / Vedic / Canaanite parallel complexes.

**Reading list embedded in the framework metadata**:
- Archie T. Wright, *The Origin of Evil Spirits* (Fortress Press, rev. ed.) — the academic anchor
- Annette Yoshiko Reed, *Fallen Angels and the History of Judaism and Christianity* (Cambridge, 2005) — the reception-history standard
- Joseph Lumpkin, *Fallen Angels, the Watchers, and the Origins of Evil* — the popular synthesis (more readable, less rigorous)
- G.A.G. Stroumsa, *Another Seed: Studies in Gnostic Mythology* (Leiden, 1984) — the Gnostic-Watcher connection

## 8. The Ugaritic / Canaanite tradition (now first-class in the database)

Adds:
- **El** — the high god, head of the divine council. Direct prototype of Hebrew El Elyon. p220 makes the identity explicit.
- **Asherah** — his consort, mother of the seventy sons of god. The "YHWH and his Asherah" of the Kuntillet Ajrud inscriptions.
- **Baal Hadad** — the storm-god, dragon-slayer, dying-and-rising god. Prototype for the death-and-resurrection pattern that later Christian theology will inherit (p218, p219).
- **Mot** — death personified.
- **Yam** — Sea, the chaos-serpent slain by Baal.
- **Anat** — warrior-virgin, sister-consort of Baal. Mourner who descends and destroys Mot.
- **The rpum** — deified royal dead / giant warriors of Bashan and Mt Hermon. **Direct linguistic and religious ancestor of the biblical Rephaim**.

## Schema notes

All new entries follow the v0.1.0 schema (deities have facets; facets have parallel_facets; canonical parallels have facet_a_id + facet_b_id + strength + type + basis + scholarly_caveat).

Strength values used in this expansion:
- **identity** — same entity under different names (e.g. El / El Elyon)
- **derivation** — one historically descended from / shares a name-root with the other (e.g. Vedic Dānu / Irish Danu)
- **strong-parallel** — same function, archetype, or structural slot (most parallels in this expansion)
- **partial-parallel** — overlap in some function-tags but not full archetype match

Where parallels are interpretive rather than historically demonstrated, a `scholarly_caveat` field marks the qualification. The user can decide how much weight to give the parallel based on the caveat.
